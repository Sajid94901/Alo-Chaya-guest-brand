1. Leadership Structure & Decision Process

Our leadership structure is not a traditional hierarchy but a "Chain of Amanah" (Trust), where responsibility flows from the **Codex (The "Why")** down to the **Practice (The "What")**.

Level Core Role (Codex Link) Primary Responsibility (HRIS Link)
Level 1: Leadership The Mirror (Why) & The Posture (Who) Set the organizational **Vision** and define the non-negotiable **CSFs (Critical Success Factors)** for the HRIS.
Level 2: Dept. Heads (MTOs) The Discipline (How) Translate CSFs into quarterly departmental **OKRs (Objectives & Key Results)**. Manage MTO/FEO performance.
Level 3: Staff (FEOs/Guides) The Practice (What) Execute daily tasks and generate the **KPIs (Key Performance Indicators)** that feed the HRIS.

The Decision-Making Flowchart

All strategic decisions must pass through this two-filter process:

1. The GLSM (Codex) Filter

The "Posture Check." Before any action, management must ask:

  • Does this align with our **Mirror (Why)**?
  • Does this violate any **Postures (CSFs)**?
  • Are we seeking **Divine Ingenuity** (the best, most ethical solution)?

2. The HRIS (Data) Filter

The "Discipline Check." After passing the GLSM, management must check the data:

  • How will this decision impact our **OKRs**?
  • What do the live **KPIs** (from the HRIS) tell us about the current situation?
  • Is this decision data-informed?

2. KPIs & Reporting Matrix

All management reporting is centralized through the **`internal-hris.html`** portal. This provides a single source of truth, allowing leadership to move from high-level CSFs down to granular KPIs.

Metric Type Definition (Codex Link) Data Source (HRIS Link) Review Frequency
CSF (Critical Success Factors) The "Posture" (Who we are). Our ethical non-negotiables. `HRIS → CSF Dashboard` Monthly (Leadership Review)
OKR (Objectives & Key Results) The "Discipline" (How we execute). Our quarterly goals. `HRIS → OKR Tracker` Bi-Weekly (Dept. Head Review)
KPI (Key Performance Indicators) The "Practice" (What we do). Our daily operational data. `HRIS → KPI Drilldown` (from PMS) Daily (Staff & Guide Review)

The Reporting Flowchart (Data Amanah)

Data flows *up* from the Practice to the Mirror, ensuring leadership decisions are based on ground-truth.

1. Daily Practice (KPIs)

**Who:** FEOs / Guides **Action:** Staff perform daily tasks (check-ins, service requests). The PMS logs this as KPIs (e.g., `Task Response Time`). **HRIS Link:** `PMS → ServiceLog` table.

2. Quarterly Discipline (OKRs)

**Who:** Dept. Heads / MTOs **Action:** Managers review the aggregated KPIs to see if they are achieving their quarterly OKRs (e.g., `Avg. rating ≥ 4.6/5`). **HRIS Link:** `OKR Tracker`.

3. Monthly Posture (CSFs)

**Who:** Leadership **Action:** Leadership reviews the OKR outcomes to ensure the core CSFs (e.g., `Service Sanctity`) are being met. **HRIS Link:** `CSF Dashboard`.
