1. The Operational Doctrine (Our Expression)

Our operations are a direct expression of our Doctrine. We use the **"3-Layer Architecture"** to manage our staff, design our jobs, and deliver our service, all of which is measured by the HRIS.

1. Oversight Layer (IGO Style)

Strategic management (The "Bureaus") sets the **OKRs** and **CSFs** that guide all operations, ensuring ethical alignment (Amanah).

2. Operational Layer (Food Wars Style)

This is how we build mastery. Jobs are designed as "skill challenges" (Shokugeki) to build the **ACEM** score, not just to complete tasks. This is linked to the **JCM**.

3. Service Layer (Konohana Kitan Style)

This is how we handle guests. Staff are trained as empathetic "Guides" to serve "Pilgrims of Light," solving the "disgruntled employee" problem with purpose.

2. Job Design: The JCM & "Dejobbing"

For prospective employees, we do not offer "jobs"; we offer "roles" built on the **Job Characteristic Model (JCM)**. Our goal is "dejobbing"—creating a flexible, highly skilled team of "Guides."

Skill Variety

You will be trained across multiple functions (e.g., a "Room Coordinator" learns F&B service) to increase your capabilities (your **ACEM score**).

Task Identity

You own the *entire* guest experience, not just one piece. A "Senior Guide" manages a guest's journey from check-in to their wellness goals.

Task Significance

Your work matters. As a "Guide" in the "Konohana Kitan" model, your role is to provide "Reassurance of Worth," a mission of real significance.

Autonomy

You are empowered by the **GLSM** to use "Divine Ingenuity" to solve guest problems on the spot, without waiting for a manager.

Feedback

You receive constant, real-time feedback from the **HRIS/PMS link**, guest reviews, and "Shokugeki" (skill challenges), not just once a year.

3. Performance: The Al-Amin Balance Scorecard

Performance is measured by the **Al-Amin Balance Scorecard**, which is the logic *inside* our internal HRIS. It balances "what" you do with "who" you are (your Posture).

The "Ghuraba Codex" (Spiritual & Wellness)

This is the foundation. We track your engagement with the **Gurabha Codex** module (Physical, Intellectual, Spiritual logs) in the HRIS as a core part of your "Posture."

The "ACEM" (Training & Growth)

This is your "Discipline." Your **Al-Amin Capability Evolution Model (ACEM)** score is tracked via completed "Shokugeki" (skill challenges) and Codex modules in the HRIS.

The "KPIs" (Daily Practice & PMS Link)

This is your "Practice." Daily actions, like `Task Response Time` or `Guest Rating`, are captured from the **PMS** and fed *directly* into the HRIS dashboard, linking your work to your performance review.

4. Operational Ranks & Job Specifications

This is the "Operational Layer" in practice. All roles are built on the "Food Wars" model of mastery, not just seniority. Below are the core roles prospective employees can apply for.

A. The Culinary Arena (Bureau of Provision)

Our F&B staffing is flexible and scenario-based. All roles are trained in the "Shokugeki" model of skill challenges.

Scenario A (Ideal): Executive Chef

A Rank 1 (Chief Dignitary) leader who oversees all culinary operations, R&D, and mentors the Sous Chefs. This role focuses on the **GLSM** (Strategic Vision).

Scenario B (Probable): Head Chef

A hands-on leader (Rank 2) who manages the daily kitchen, runs the "Skill Challenge" system, and is supported by a **Sous Chef Trainer** (who trains new Apprentices).

Core Culinary Staff (All Scenarios)

The "Dignified Staff" (Rank 3) of the kitchen:

  • Line Chef: Master of a specific station (e.g., Saucier, Garde Manger).
  • Commis Chef: A junior chef supporting a station.
  • Gastronomic Technician: A specialist in food science, presentation, or ethical sourcing (a key "Dignified" role).

B. Dignified Staff (Harmony, Sanctity & Clarity)

These are the core "Dignified Staff" (Rank 3) and "Guides" (Rank 2/4) who run the guest-facing and estate operations, based on the "Konohana Kitan" model of service.

Room Coordinator (Bureau of Harmony)

The dignified name for our housekeeping staff. They are "coordinators" of the guest's personal sanctuary, trained in the "Iyashi (Soothing) Policy" and "Anticipatory Kindness."

Landscaper (Bureau of Provision)

The dignified name for our estate and grounds crew. They are the custodians of the "Riverine Seclusion" and are responsible for the aesthetic beauty and safety of the environment.

Senior Guide (Bureau of Clarity)

A Rank 2 leader responsible for mentoring new staff and leading guest experiences (e.g., "Dialogue Sessions" in the Intellectual Arena, "Trial of the Nafs" kayaking).

Apprentice Guide (All Bureaus)

A Rank 4 trainee. This is the entry point for all new hires. Their primary goal is to complete the core Codex modules and pass their first "Skill Challenge" to become Dignified Staff.
